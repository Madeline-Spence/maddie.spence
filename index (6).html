Core Values & Character

How I See Myself

I see myself as energetic, outgoing, hardworking, and genuinely supportive. I lead with positivity and believe that a strong team culture begins with each individual's attitude and commitment to showing up fully.

How Others See Me

Feedback consistently described me as super positive, easy to talk to, a great listener, and someone who helps others feel at ease. I am seen as a reliable and encouraging presence in any group setting.

Gap Insight

I tend to be harder on myself than others are. The gap here is not a conflict — it is that others recognize strengths in me that I sometimes minimize or overlook. They see my value more clearly than I allow myself to.

Moving Forward

I want to trust how other people see me more fully, and step more confidently into leadership. If others already see these qualities in me, my next step is to own them — and act from that place of grounded self-assurance.

◐
Communication Style

How I See Myself

I see myself as a thoughtful communicator who genuinely listens. At the same time, I recognize that I tend to overthink my delivery and sometimes hold back — particularly in situations that involve conflict or direct confrontation.

How Others See Me

Others describe me as confident, outgoing, and easy to talk to. I am seen as someone who communicates clearly and makes people feel heard. My external presence reads as far more assured than I often feel internally.

Gap Insight

Internally, I feel more uncertain than I appear to others. The confidence others perceive is real — but I do not always feel it from the inside. This gap revealed that my hesitation lives mostly in my own thinking, not in my actual performance.

Moving Forward

I want to be more direct, take more initiative in difficult conversations, and trust my instincts rather than second-guessing my words. My goal is for my internal clarity to fully match the confident communication style others already see in me.

◌
Work Environment & Team Culture

How I See Myself

I thrive in positive, supportive environments where people communicate openly and work toward shared goals. Collaboration is where I feel most energized, and I naturally invest in the culture of any team I am part of.

How Others See Me

Others see me as someone who actively builds that environment — bringing positive energy, lifting team morale, and creating a space where everyone feels comfortable and valued for their contributions.

Gap Insight

This dimension showed the strongest alignment of the three. What I value in a team culture, I am already embodying in practice. That consistency between identity and reputation is something I am proud of and committed to sustaining.

Moving Forward

I want to be even more intentional about inclusion — ensuring that all voices are heard and that every team member feels genuinely comfortable contributing. I see this as the natural next level of the team culture I already help create.

Growth Over the Semester

This semester deepened my understanding of how identity and reputation intersect. I became more intentional about the way I communicate, more aware of the gap between how I feel internally and how I actually show up, and more committed to leading with consistency. The most meaningful shift was recognizing that confidence — for me — means trusting what others already see.

Moving Forward

Going forward, I aim to step more fully into leadership, communicate with greater directness, and continue building a professional presence that is as clear and grounded on the inside as it appears on the outside. I want to carry the insights from this semester into every professional environment I enter — with intention, authenticity, and confidence.
